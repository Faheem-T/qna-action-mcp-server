### 1. Definitions
**Insured**: The person whose life is covered by this policy.
**Owner**: The person or entity who owns the policy and has the rights to exercise the privileges of ownership.
**Beneficiary**: The person or entity designated to receive the death benefit.
**Policy Date**: The date the policy becomes effective.
**Face Amount**: The amount of insurance coverage purchased.
**Cash Value**: The accumulated savings element of the policy.

### 2. Insuring Agreement
We agree to pay the Death Benefit to the Beneficiary upon receipt of due proof of the Insured's death, subject to the provisions of this policy. The death must occur while this policy is in force.

### 3. Ownership and Beneficiary
**Rights of Owner**: While the insured is living, the Owner may exercise all rights and privileges provided by this policy.
**Change of Beneficiary**: The Owner may change the Beneficiary at any time during the Insured's lifetime by written request.
**Designation**: If no primary beneficiary survives the Insured, the proceeds will be paid to the contingent beneficiary.

### 4. Premiums and Grace Period
**Payment**: Premiums are payable in advance at our Home Office or to an authorized agent.
**Grace Period**: A grace period of 31 days will be allowed for payment of each premium after the first. If death occurs during the grace period, the unpaid premium will be deducted from the proceeds.

### 5. Reinstatement
If this policy lapses due to non-payment of premium, it may be reinstated within 5 years upon:
1. Written application.
2. Evidence of insurability satisfactory to us.
3. Payment of all overdue premiums with interest.

### 6. Incontestability
Terms regarding the validity of the policy after it has been in force for two years from the Policy Date.

### 7. Suicide Exclusion
If the Insured commits suicide, while sane or insane, within two years from the Policy Date, our liability will be limited to a refund of the premiums paid.

### 8. Misstatement of Age or Gender
If the Insured's age or gender has been misstated, the amount payable will be what the premiums paid would have purchased at the correct age and gender.

### 9. Settlement Options
The proceeds may be paid in a lump sum or under one of the following options:
- **Option A**: Interest Income.
- **Option B**: Installments for a fixed period.
- **Option C**: Life Income.
- **Option D**: Installments of a fixed amount.

### 10. Policy Loans
The Owner may borrow against the Cash Value of the policy at an annual interest rate of 5%. Failure to repay the loan may cause the policy to lapse if the indebtedness exceeds the Cash Value.

### 11. Surrender Value
The Owner may surrender the policy for its Net Cash Value. The Net Cash Value is the Cash Value less any policy loans and interest.

### 12. Dividend Options
(Participating policies only)
Annual dividends may be:
- Paid in cash.
- Applied to reduce premiums.
- Used to purchase paid-up additions.
- Left to accumulate at interest.

### 13. Riders and Endorsements
**Accidental Death Benefit Rider**: Pays an additional benefit if death is accidental.
**Waiver of Premium Rider**: Waives premiums if the Insured becomes totally disabled.
**Children's Term Rider**: Provides term insurance on the Insured's children.

### 14. General Provisions
**Contract**: This policy and the application constitute the entire contract.
**Modification**: No agent has authority to change this policy or waive any of its provisions.
**Assignment**: The Owner may assign this policy. We are not responsible for the validity of any assignment.
