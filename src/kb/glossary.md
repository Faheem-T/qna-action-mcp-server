# Insurance Glossary

## A
**Actuary**: A professional who analyzes statistical data to estimate risks and set premiums.
**Adjuster**: A representative who investigates claims and determines the amount payable.

## B
**Beneficiary**: The person or entity designated to receive the death benefit.
**Binder**: A temporary contract of insurance.

## C
**Cash Value**: The savings element of a permanent life insurance policy.
**Co-payment**: A fixed amount paid by the insured for a covered service.

## D
**Deductible**: The amount the insured must pay out-of-pocket before the insurance company pays.
**Dividend**: A return of part of the premium to the policyholder (in participating policies).

## E
**Exclusion**: A provision in the policy that eliminates coverage for certain risks.

## P
**Premium**: The amount paid for insurance coverage.
**Policy**: The contract between the insured and the insurer.

## R
**Rider**: An amendment to the policy that adds or restricts coverage.

## U
**Underwriting**: The process of evaluating risk to determine insurability and premium rates.
